Vegan & Dairy-Free Cinnamon Rolls

Description

Warm, gooey, and perfectly spiced, these vegan cinnamon rolls are the ultimate comfort bake. Made with rich coconut milk and naturally sweetened with coconut sugar, they’re soft, fluffy, and packed with cozy cinnamon flavor in every bite.

The filling is lightly caramelized and warmly spiced, while the creamy frosting on top can be made fully vegan or traditional. Perfect for brunch, dessert, or a slow weekend morning.

  • 2 ¼ tsp yeast
  • 1 ¼ cup warmed canned coconut milk
  • 3 tbsp coconut sugar
  • 3 tbsp oil
  • 3 cups flour
  • 1 tsp vanilla

Filling

  • 3 tbsp softened vegan butter
  • ¾ cup coconut sugar or brown sugar
  • 1 small banana, mashed
  • 2 tbsp cinnamon
  • Optional: 1 tbsp flour if the mixture is too runny

Frosting

  • ½ stick vegan butter
  • ½ stick cream cheese (vegan or regular)
  • 1–2 cups powdered sugar, to taste
  • Dash of salt
  • 1 tsp vanilla

Instructions

  1. Activate the yeast
    2.

    In a small bowl, mix the warmed coconut milk with yeast and coconut sugar. Let it sit for 10 minutes until foamy and bubbly—this ensures your rolls are light and fluffy.

  2. Make the dough
    3.

    In a stand mixer, combine the flour. Pour in the yeast mixture, oil, and vanilla. Mix until a dough forms, then knead for about 5 minutes until smooth and elastic. Cover and let rise in a warm place for 1 hour.

  3. Roll out the dough
    4.

    On a lightly floured surface, roll the dough into a rectangle. Spread the filling evenly, then roll it up tightly into a log.

  4. Slice and proof
    5.

    Use floss or string to slice the rolled dough into individual rolls. Place them in a greased baking pan, cover, and let rise for 30 minutes to 1 hour.

  5. Bake
    6.

    Preheat the oven to 350°F (175°C). Bake the rolls for 15–20 minutes, or until lightly golden. Let cool for about 10 minutes.

  6. Frost and serve
    7.

    Mix the frosting ingredients until smooth and creamy. Spread over warm rolls and enjoy!

Tips & Tricks

  • Banana substitution: You can leave out the banana for a more traditional filling, or swap for applesauce.
  • Sweetness: Adjust sugar in the filling or frosting to your taste.
  • Storage: Store leftovers in an airtight container at room temperature for up to 2 days or freeze for later.
