Apple Pie

Description

A timeless classic, this apple pie combines a flaky double‑crust lattice top with tender, juicy Granny Smith apples and a buttery sugar sauce. Perfect for holidays, family dinners, or any cozy dessert craving.

Ingredients

  • 1 (9‑inch) double‑crust pie pastry, thawed
  • 8 small Granny Smith apples, peeled, cored, and sliced
  • ½ cup unsalted butter
  • 3 tablespoons all‑purpose flour
  • ½ cup white sugar
  • ½ cup packed brown sugar
  • ¼ cup water

Instructions

  1. Preheat and prepare
    2.

    Preheat your oven to 425°F (220°C). Place one pie crust into a 9‑inch pie pan, pressing it gently into the sides.

  2. Make the sugar sauce
    3.

    In a saucepan over medium heat, melt the butter. Stir in the flour to form a paste, then add the white sugar, brown sugar, and water. Bring the mixture to a boil, then reduce the heat and simmer for 3–5 minutes.

  3. Assemble the pie
    4.

    Add the sliced apples into the bottom crust, mounding them slightly. Arrange the second crust as a lattice top over the apples.

  4. Add sauce and bake
    5.

    Slowly pour the sugar and butter mixture over the lattice crust, making sure it seeps into the pie. Bake for 15 minutes at 425°F (220°C), then reduce the oven temperature to 350°F (175°C) and bake for another 35– 45 minutes, until apples are soft and crust is golden.

  5. Cool
    6.

    Remove the pie from the oven and let it cool before slicing.

Serving Suggestions

  • Classic: Serve each slice warm with a scoop of vanilla ice cream on the side — the contrast of hot pie and cold ice cream is delightful.
  • Extra cozy: Sprinkle a dash of ground cinnamon or nutmeg over the ice cream for added warm spice notes.

Tips & Tricks

  • Apple choice: Granny Smith apples offer tartness and hold their shape well, but you can mix in Honeycrisp or Fuji for a sweeter bite.
  • Lattice ease: Chill the dough slightly if it gets too warm while weaving the lattice crust.
  • Storage: Cover leftover pie and refrigerate for up to 3–4 days; reheat slices briefly in a warm oven.
